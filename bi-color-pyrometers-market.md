<p><p>Bi-color pyrometers are temperature measurement devices that use the principle of color ratio to determine the temperature of an object without contact. The market features two primary types: optical and infrared pyrometers. Optical pyrometers rely on visible light emitted by hot objects and measure color hues to estimate temperature. In contrast, infrared pyrometers detect infrared radiation, which is emitted by all objects with a temperature above absolute zero, providing accurate readings across various applications, including industrial processes and research.</p></p>
