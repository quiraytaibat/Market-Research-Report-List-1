<p><p>Compounding pharmacy services encompass two primary types: aseptic and non-sterile compounding. Aseptic compounding involves the preparation of sterile medications, typically for patients requiring injections or infusions, ensuring that the products are free from contaminants. This process adheres to stringent regulations to maintain safety. In contrast, non-sterile compounding involves creating medications in forms like creams, capsules, or tablets that do not require sterility. Both categories cater to personalized patient needs, addressing specific therapeutic requirements not met by commercial pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
