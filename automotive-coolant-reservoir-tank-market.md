<p><p>The automotive coolant reservoir tank market is divided into pressurized and non-pressurized types. Pressurized tanks are designed to withstand higher pressures, enhancing coolant efficiency and preventing boiling, which makes them suitable for modern vehicles with advanced cooling systems. Non-pressurized tanks, on the other hand, operate at ambient pressure and are simpler in design, typically found in older vehicles or those with basic cooling mechanisms. Each type serves specific vehicle requirements, impacting performance and maintenance practices.</p></p>
